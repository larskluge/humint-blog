    Exploring Like a BlockGenius

    by Alex Preukschat

    Alex is the Bitcoin editor of the Spanish financial website OroyFinanzas.com, and is working on a @BitcoinComic to be published in October 2014.

    While most of the media attention surrounding Bitcoin and other cryptocurrencies relates to either the price or the latest hack or theft, the most exciting aspect is undoubtedly its technology, of which the blockchain is the primary construct. The blockchain was created as a part of the Bitcoin protocol and is its key innovation, allowing the secure exchange of tokens in a decentralized ledger of consensus without the need for a central authority.

    Blockchain explorers like Blockchain.info, Blockexplorer and Blockr.io have done fundamental work to spread a broader understanding of what the blockchain is and how it can be used. Thanks to these services, the contents of the blockchain are now easily traversable by everyone, allowing us to concern ourselves with the question of what is missing in the ecosystem.

    As cryptocurrency grows and matures, we should embrace all efforts to make the blockchain ever more accessible, with the goal of inching ourselves closer to a fundamentally more useful, educational and even fun presentation of its contents. Such attention to detail can foster a broader interest in the importance of cryptography in protecting our privacy, the "meaning" of money, and the overall potential of decentralized peer-to-peer technologies.

    Luckily there are plenty of ways to bring Bitcoin and the blockchain closer to the hearts and minds of its users. In the following examples I utilize a fictional site called BlockGenius to explore some of these ideas.

    Making the Blockchain Social

    A new block in Bitcoin is mined on average every ten minutes. Each of these blocks, and the transactions within them, is relevant to the history of Bitcoin. From events of historical import like the purchase of pizza by Lazlo in Florida for 10,000 Bitcoin, to a personal annotation of the blockchain as related to events of our own lives, all can be relevant using the blockchain as a reference point to share with others.

    As expected from its namesake, each block could be organized so that users could annotate content in a manner similar to RapGenius. Instead of having users comment on songs or news articles, the blocks of all relevant cryptocurrencies would be available for annotation. For the privacy conscious, BlockGenius would even allow annotation in a manner visible only to friends and family.

    Users of BlockGenius could vote up or down annotations for each block, publicly or privately. High quality annotations could be rewarded with cryptocurrencies by the community and the publisher of the site — perhaps through an internal cryptocurrency or a social media friendly coin like Dogecoin. Rewarding users that contribute value would also constitute a basic public reputation system.

    Celebrity Blocks

    Since Satoshi Nakamoto and other individuals in the cryptocurrency space made the blockchain and related technologies possible, we could celebrate them in BlockGenius with designs and special content contributed by the community. BlockGenius would highlight special pages with a special design and special content that can be voted on. For example, the Genesis Block page could pay tribute to Satoshi Nakamoto, and designers could submit their designs to the community to be used as a background image for the specific public URL (e.g. BlockGenius/bitcoin/block/0-genesis-block).

    What about a GUI?

    Too often, many aspects of the Bitcoin and cryptocurrency ecosystem are made exclusively by technically-minded individuals, yet if we want to bring cryptocurrencies closer to the mainstream, we must continue to work together to make it even more accesible to everyone.

    The different elements that compose the blockchain can be made graphically unique, and BlockGenius would share easy-to-digest content allowing anyone to gradually build up knowledge of how the blockchain is composed. The content could be outlined and constructed by the community in a fun and educational way, ultimately being edited by user vote.

    In summary, with a "genius" approach, the blockchain will continue to provide many more amazing outlets for creativity and historical reference, ultimately playing its own small part in making the world a better and more transparent place to live.
